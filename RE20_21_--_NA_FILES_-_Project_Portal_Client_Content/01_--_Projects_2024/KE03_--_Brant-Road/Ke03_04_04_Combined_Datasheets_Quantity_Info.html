General Note | Methodology

Volumetric Data Capture:

  • A theoretical 10 m² area of wall was modeled for each masonry type: Metric Brickwork, Imperial Gauge Brickwork, and Standard Blockwork.

  • Each wall section was set at 5 meters in length and 2 meters in height.

  • The gross volume was calculated by modeling the overall wall shell and subtracting the volume of bricks or blocks extruded to the specified thickness (e.g., 100 mm for brickwork).

  • This process resulted in two separate volume shells:

    • One representing the brickwork or blockwork.
    • One representing the gross mortar volume.

  • The mortar volume was further divided into sub-volumes representing sand and cement based on the specified mix ratios (1:4, 1:5, and 1:6).

Data Extraction:

  • A custom Ruby script, NS_Volume_Data_Extractor.rb, was used to extract precise volumetric data from the SketchUp models.
  • The extracted data was tabulated and refined for analysis.

Volume Calculations

  • Individual Unit Volumes:

    • Calculated based on standard dimensions:

      • Metric Brick: 215 mm × 100 mm × 65 mm.
      • Imperial Gauge Brick: 215 mm × 100 mm × 73 mm.
      • Standard Block: 440 mm × 100 mm × 215 mm.

    • Note: The brick width for Imperial Gauge Brickwork has been corrected to 100 mm to maintain consistency with standard dimensions. All calculations have been updated accordingly.

Total Volumes

  • Total brick/block volume for 10 m² of wall.
  • Mortar volume calculated by subtracting brick/block volume from the gross wall volume.

Material Weights

  • Calculated using standard material densities:

    • Cement Density: 1,440 kg/m³.
    • Sand Density: 1,600 kg/m³.

  • Weights for a 10 m² wall section were calculated and then divided by 10 to obtain per-square-meter values.

Assumptions

  • All bricks and blocks are assumed to be uniform in size and shape.
  • Mortar joints are assumed to be consistent throughout the wall.
  • Densities are based on dry materials without accounting for moisture content.
  • Mortar mix ratios are strictly adhered to (1:4, 1:5, 1:6) for cement to sand proportions.

Metrics and Units:

  • Volumes (Vol 10 m³):

    • Represent the total volume of each material required for a 10 m² wall area.

  • Weights:

    • 10 m² Wall: Weight of materials required for a 10 m² wall section.
    • 1 m² Wall: Derived by dividing the 10 m² values by 10.

  • Standard Densities Used:

    • Cement: 1,440 kg/m³.
    • Sand: 1,600 kg/m³.

Wastage and Variations

  • Calculations do not account for material wastage or on-site variations.
  • A typical wastage factor of 5–10% is recommended to be added to material estimates.

Simplifications & Limitations Of This Study & Dataset

  • Theoretical models assume ideal conditions and do not account for practical construction variables such as:
  • The impact of moisture on material weights is not considered. Actual weights may vary due to moisture in materials.
  • Variations in mortar joint thickness.
  • Irregularities in brick/block dimensions.
  • Workmanship quality.
  • Account for moisture content in materials, which can affect actual weights.
  • Include a wastage factor (typically 5–10%) in material estimates to accommodate on-site losses and errors.

Masonry Mortar | Material Properties

NA05_02_04_Mortar_Material_Properties

Masonry Mortar Core Constituents

  • Aggregate | Soft building sand is used in masonry mortar to ensure a smooth and workable mix, free from stones and other debris.
  • Cement | Acts as the binding agent. The ratio of cement to sand affects the mortar's strength and setting time.
  • Additives | Plasticisers improve workability. Accelerators speed up setting time. Frost additives help mortar cure in cold conditions.

In Detail | Soft Building Sand

Sand Weight | Simplified

The weight of Soft Sand per cubic meter (m³) depends on its moisture content and type, but the typical values are:

  • Dry Soft Sand | Weighs around : 1600 kg per 1m³
  • Wet Soft Sand | Can weigh up to : 1900 kg per m³

Typical Packaging Sizes

  • Bulk Trade Size | Typically sold in 850 kg to 1,000 kg (1 tonne) bags
  • Retail Size Bags | Commonly available in 25 kg bags

Sand Weight | Recognised International Standards

British Standard (BSI)

The British Standard for building sand is specified in BS 1199 and BS 1200, which set the requirements for sand used in masonry mortars. The standard density for dry building sand is approximately 1,600 kg/m³. This figure is typically used in project planning and material estimation throughout the UK construction industry.

European Standards

In the Eurocode, no specific separate density for soft sand is stated. However, the standard densities across individual states in Europe align closely with British standards, generally ranging between 1,500 kg/m³ to 1,700 kg/m³. This range accounts for variations in sand composition and type.

American Standard (ASTM)

The American Standard for sand density is governed by ASTM C778, which focuses on the grading and density requirements of sands used in hydraulic cement testing. The typical density for dry sand, similar to the British and European standards, is about 1,600 kg/m³.

International Organisation For Standardisation (ISO)

ISO 11272 deals with the measurement of dry bulk density for both soil and sand. It also specifies a typical density for dry sands around 1,600 kg/m³, consistent with the other standards used worldwide.

Cement Weight | Recognised International Standards

British Standard (BSI)

The British Standard for cement is outlined in BS EN 197-1, which defines the composition, specifications, and conformity criteria for 27 distinct types of common cements. The standard density for dry cement used in construction is approximately 1,440 kg/m³

European Standards

The Eurocode adopts similar standards, with cement density generally ranging between 1,400 kg/m³ to 1,450 kg/m³, depending on the specific type of cement and its constituents. This aligns closely with British standards

American Standard (ASTM)

The ASTM C150 provides the American specification for Portland cement. The typical density for dry cement in the US is also 1,440 kg/m³, comparable to the UK and European standard.

International Organization for Standardization (ISO)

The ISO 679 standard, which deals with cement testing, provides consistent figures for cement density, typically ranging from 1,400 to 1,450 kg/m³ in line with other global standards.
